Aashiq Hill

Born on the 17th of August 1989 in Cape Town, South Africa, his love for knowledge began when he was a high school student and started attending classes at the masjid.

He enrolled at an institution for higher Islamic studies and studied all the basic Islamic sciences & graduated six years later (2006-2011) at the age of 21. Besides his formal studies, he also studied privately with various scholars around Cape Town as well as online. He covered the following subjects: Hadith, the science of Hadith, Aqeedah and Fiqh, Usul al-Fiqh and Seerah, with private teachers.

Currently, he is enrolled as a BAIS student at IOU whilst simultaneously completing a B.A (Hons) degree in Theology at the University of the Western Cape. He taught various Islamic sciences for three years at the institution where he graduated from. Apart from that he teaches various private classes (Hadith, Arabic, Fiqh, Aqeedah etc) at different venues around Cape Town. He is also a regular guest lecturer at different mosques in Cape Town and a lecturer for the Al-Huda foundation (an institution offering short courses, seminars and various classes).

Currently residing in Cape Town, he is married and has 2 daughters.





Ather Khan

An electronics & communications engineer by professional training who opted to spread the word of Allah”, is an apt description of this 32 year old public speaker & trainer, His journey into the world of comparative religion & Da’wah started at the age of 21 as a participant of the Da’wah Training Programme conducted by Dr. Zakir Naik at IRF, Mumbai in the year 2001.
 
The zeal within him in clarifying misconceptions about Islam was kindled by Shaikh Ahmed Deedat & Dr. Zakir Naik. The exposure to these world renowned scholars resulted in him choosing Da’wah over engineering. Being a passionate full time Da’ee since 2002, specializing in inter-faith dialogues, Ather Khan has participated in numerous one-on-one sessions & group discussions with non-muslims and, (by the grace of Allah) has clarified a number of misconceptions about Islam amongst television viewers as well as live audiences in various parts of the globe.

He has been delivering talks on Islam and Comparative Religion for the last ten years.

Ather Khan is also an accomplished trainer with experience in imparting Da’wah training to students of comparative religion and was a faculty member in the IRF Da’wah Training Programme conducted in 2004 and he also conducted a Da’wah Training Programme organized by the IPCI, Birmingham (UK).
